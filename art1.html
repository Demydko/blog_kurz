Page Turner

The transition from page to screen is always exciting, but there's nothing quite like experiencing a story in its original form. Here are ten books you should add to your reading list before they hit the big screen: Dune by Frank Herbert – A sci-fi epic that's as relevant today as when it was first published. Where the Crawdads Sing by Delia Owens – A beautifully written mystery set in the swamps of North Carolina. The Night Circus by Erin Morgenstern – A magical tale of a traveling circus that appears only at night. The Goldfinch by Donna Tartt – A Pulitzer Prize-winning novel that explores art, loss, and identity. The Last Thing He Told Me by Laura Dave – A gripping thriller about family secrets and deception. Anatomy of a Scandal by Sarah Vaughan – A courtroom drama that delves into the lives of the elite. A Man Called Ove by Fredrik Backman – A heartwarming story about an irritable old man and his unlikely friendships. The Underground Railroad by Colson Whitehead – A powerful reimagining of history that won the Pulitzer Prize. Project Hail Mary by Andy Weir – From the author of The Martian, this sci-fi novel is full of twists and turns. The Ballad of Songbirds and Snakes by Suzanne Collins – A prequel to The Hunger Games that dives into the backstory of President Snow. Make sure to read these before the lights dim and the opening credits roll!
